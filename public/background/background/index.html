Project Background

October 2, 2025 · 2 min
Table of Contents
  • Project Goals
  • Current Setup (as of October 2025)
  • What to Expect Here

Ever since I sold my E36 convertible and moved into a modern BMW (F30 330i), I’ve found myself missing that classic BMW straight-six character. So I went hunting on Facebook Marketplace and came across this 2004 325Ci.

It wasn’t my first choice, but with a tight budget and the usual Marketplace sellers who don’t like to respond, I went for it. It’s been in an accident, the interior was falling apart, the water pump was leaking, and there were plenty of oil leaks — classic BMW problems.

It’s been a couple of months since I bought it, and most of those issues are now fixed. Right now it sits in the driveway with a welded diff and worn-out rear tires, after a couple sideways mishaps, because I briefly lost sight of what I wanted to do with this car. But now I know — and this blog will serve as my logbook to keep track of everything along the way.

(Sitting next to the retired drift sedan)

Project Goals#

  • Keep it reliable enough for daily use
  • Improve handling and responsiveness
  • Add tasteful performance mods without going overboard
  • Document every step with photos, parts lists, and notes

Current Setup (as of October 2025)#

  • Engine: Stock M54B25
  • Exhaust: Stock w/ muffler delete
  • Differential: Welded
  • Suspension: Maxpeeding rods coilovers

What to Expect Here#

Each update, whether it’s a simple repair or a major modification — will get its own post.

Posts will include:

  • Purpose of the repair/mod
  • Parts & tools used
  • Photos before/during/after
  • Results and lessons learned
  • Tags for easy searching (engine, suspension, interior, etc.)

Thanks for following along — whether you’re here for E46 inspiration, how-to help, or just looking
