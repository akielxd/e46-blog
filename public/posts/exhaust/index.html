Custom Single Exit Exhaust

October 8, 2025 · 2 min
Table of Contents
  • Removal

Today’s the day I finally make this car louder — and sound less like a referee.
At high RPM, it legit whistled like a kettle. I couldn’t deal with it any longer.

I found a set of catless headers and a Y-pipe on Facebook Marketplace for $50 — insane deal.
To make it even sweeter, the seller threw in a custom single-exit exhaust, already welded and ready to go.

I’m not a fan of that infamous M54 rasp, though — so I’ll eventually add a resonator or two and a muffler to balance tone and volume.

Removal#

Here’s the starting point. My windshield washer reservoir and SAP pump were already deleted and honestly, that made life easier since both would’ve needed to come out for header clearance anyway.

After jacking up the car, I started at the connection between the headers and the rest of the exhaust, four rusty bolts that definitely put up a fight.
I managed to remove them all without cutting, but two broke in the process. Still that’s a win in my book.

Next, I moved toward the mid-section bracket, which holds the exhaust with rubber hangers.
Pretty cool design, hadn’t seen it done like this before.

With the bracket off and rear hangers unhooked, the entire system slid right out.
Heavier than I expected, but it wasnt too bad.

You’ll notice this wasn’t a fully stock exhaust, it had a muffler delete, which might’ve been the source of that high-pitched whistle.

Now for the fun part, the headers.
This ended up being the hardest part of the whole job. The top bolts were manageable, but the bottom ones?
Nightmare.

Had to drop the front subframe and remove the passenger-side engine mount just to reach them.
After a few busted knuckles and creative words, they finally came free.
